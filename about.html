About Me

My name is Akash Patney, and I am currently a student at the University of Michigan majoring in computer science engineering and minoring in mathematics.

Similar to most kids, growing up I had no concrete idea in my mind, as it relates to what exact career I would choose. I knew that, in school, I tended to be drawn more towards quantitative subjects, so - when I came to university - I decided to to pursue courses in computer science and mathematics.

Over the last few years, my education has contributed greatly towards my appreciation for both the practical and theoretical components of computer science, which often are established through a foundation of rigorous mathematics. In addition, I have taken coursework in several humanities courses, which I believe have been critical towards maintaining a well rounded perspective about the world.

Ultimately, my joy for computer science stems from one particular aspect: the ease of creation. Studying computer science has equipped me with the skills to take an idea and launch it to become a tangible product almost seamlessly. This, I believe, to be the primary reason why computer science has the power to be so impactful. There is certainly a steep learning curve, but a strong foundation in data structures and algorithms opens infinitely many possibilities.

The projects that I have worked on continue to grow in complexity, but it is the same excitement for seeing a project function as intended that motivates me to continue pursuing computer science. Studying computer science has shown me how many useful applications can be created with just a computer, and I think that is one of the most unique facets of computer science.

Outside of academics, my primary interests tend to revolve around being active. I enjoy going to the gym, playing intramural sports offered through the University of Michigan, and spending time exploring new places.
